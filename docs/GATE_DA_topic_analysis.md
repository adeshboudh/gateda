Source: official question papers (IISc Bengaluru 2024, IIT Roorkee 2025, IIT Guwahati 2026) and the official DA syllabus.

## 1. Paper structure (fixed across all 3 years)

| Section               | Questions | Marks each | Section marks |
| --------------------- | --------- | ---------- | ------------- |
| General Aptitude (GA) | Q1–Q5     | 1          | 5             |
| General Aptitude (GA) | Q6–Q10    | 2          | 10            |
| DA core — 1 mark      | Q11–Q35   | 1          | 25            |
| DA core — 2 marks     | Q36–Q65   | 2          | 60            |

## 5. Key takeaways

1. **Probability & Statistics is the single highest-weight topic** (avg ~21.6%, rising every year). Subtopics seen repeatedly: Bayes theorem, conditional expectation, exponential/normal/Bernoulli distributions, CDF/PDF reasoning, CLT, joint pdfs, variance, covariance.

2. **Top-4 bucket (PS + PDSA + ML + DBW) consistently delivers ~70%+ of DA marks**:
   - 2024: 57/85 = 67%
   - 2025: 58/85 = 68%
   - 2026: 67/85 = 79%

3. **DBW weightage jumped sharply in 2026** (7 → 11 → 18). 2026 added more SQL nested queries, B+ tree indexing, ER-to-relational, OLAP cuboids, tuple calculus. Worth heavier prep.

4. **PDSA dropped from 20 (2024) to 14 (2025, 2026)** but stays a top-3 topic. Recurring: recursion tracing, sorting comparisons, binary search recurrences, tree traversals, graph traversal (BFS/DFS), Python semantics (mutable defaults, closures, list ops).

5. **Machine Learning is rock-steady at ~14–15 marks/year**. Recurring: SVM, clustering (k-means, single/complete linkage, hierarchical), PCA, ridge/linear regression, naive Bayes, KNN, neural networks (gradients, ReLU, MLP param count), decision-tree info gain, classifier evaluation (precision/recall/accuracy).

6. **Linear Algebra holds 8–12 marks/year**. Recurring: eigenvalues, projection/orthogonal/idempotent matrices, SVD, rank/nullity, quadratic forms, system-of-equations solvability.

7. **AI sits at the lower end (6–10 marks)** but appears every year — A\*, alpha-beta pruning, minimax game trees, first-order/propositional logic, Bayesian-network inference (variable elimination, sampling).

8. **Calculus & Optimization collapsed in 2026** (8 → 9 → 2 marks). Still useful for cross-topic reasoning (gradients in ML, limits, derivative continuity), but stand-alone questions are shrinking.

## 7. Classification notes / caveats

- A few questions straddle two buckets (e.g., Bayesian-network probability computations could go under PS or AI; state-space BFS could go under PDSA or AI). The classification above places each question under its **dominant** syllabus topic to keep totals at exactly 85 marks per year.
- "Counting (permutations and combinations)" is officially under Probability and Statistics in the GATE DA syllabus, so combinatorial questions (e.g., 2026 Q33 bijections of {1,2,3,4}) are counted under PS.
- General Aptitude (15 marks/year) is excluded from this topic-weightage analysis since it is not part of the DA subject syllabus.
