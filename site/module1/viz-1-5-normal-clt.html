Visualization 2 — Central Limit Theorem Demo

Sample n values from any distribution. Compute the sample mean $\bar{X}$. Repeat. Watch the distribution of $\bar{X}$ converge to Normal — regardless of the source distribution.

CLT
$$\bar{X}_n = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n X_i \xrightarrow{d} \mathcal{N}\!\left(\mu,\,\frac{\sigma^2}{n}\right) \text{ as } n\to\infty$$

GATE Angle

Standard Normal: Know Φ(1.96)≈0.975, Φ(1.645)≈0.95, Φ(2.576)≈0.995. Most GATE Normal questions use these.

Z-score: Z = (X−μ)/σ. GATE gives μ, σ, asks P(X ≤ x) → convert to Z → lookup table.

CLT in GATE: "Sample of n=36 from population μ=100, σ=12 — find P(X̄ ≥ 102)" → SE=12/6=2, Z=(102−100)/2=1, P = 1−Φ(1) ≈ 0.159.
